# Industry Consensus · 2026-04

## 1. Q1'26 Earnings Confirm the AI Capex Supercycle — and Expose Who Is Really Monetizing It

**Established:** 2026-04 (Q1'26 earnings season)
**Sources:** INTEL_20260424_0406.pdf, JPM_Intel_Better_Q1_Q2_o_2026-04-24_5276341.pdf, 1.AMZN Everyone wants this to work....pdf, 7.Oracle Corporation…Computing the Upside – And the Cost.pdf, 2404 科技日报 英.PDF

The Q1'26 print across hyperscalers and the semi stack reinforces that AI capex is still accelerating, but also sharpens the separation between companies directly monetizing inference (AWS, Oracle GPUaS, NVIDIA-proximate analog/power) and those caught in a squeeze (enterprise SaaS, HDD/NAND second-tier names). AWS disclosed AI run-rate >$15B (up from ~$10B one quarter earlier), Oracle GPUaS peak-capex guidance implies ~$325B through CY27, Intel DC&AI revenue grew +22% Y/Y, and TSLA raised 2026 capex by $5B to >$25B. Meanwhile SaaS names that missed or softened guides (NOW −17%, CRM −5%, WDAY −9%, TEAM −9%) sold off hard, signaling the market is no longer paying up for "AI-washing."

## 2. Power / Analog Semis Have Become a Second-Wave AI Trade (TXN, STM, IFX Leading)

**Established:** 2026-04
**Sources:** 2404 科技日报 英.PDF (Mizuho note on STM), 10.Texas Instruments (TXN) Q126 recap (Bernstein)

Analog and power semiconductor suppliers — previously treated as a late-cycle industrial trade — have re-rated in April on explicit guidance that AI data center power delivery is the next leg. TXN +18% on beat-and-raise, with a broad analog rally (MCHP +9.5%, ON +9.5%, NXPI +7%, ADI +5%). Mizuho estimates STM's 2029E AI DC growth at +50%+ Y/Y with $235K/GW unit economics driving an incremental ~$48B TAM, including 600V power and AWS silicon photonics ramps into 2H26. TXN, STM, and IFX are the three names investors are consistently naming as the preferred vehicles for the power-theme thesis.

## 3. High-NA EUV Adoption Has Slipped to 2028+ — Reshapes Litho Investment Narrative

**Established:** 2026-04-22 (TSMC Technology Symposium)
**Sources:** 2.Quick Take ASML - High-NA EUV adoption postponed.pdf (Bernstein)

TSMC's annual Technology Symposium on April 22 confirmed High-NA EUV will not be adopted through 2029. A14 arrives in 2028 and A13/A12 in 2029, with DRAM (D0a, 2027–2028) and logic at A10 (2030) as the first HNA adopters — not memory leading the way, as some had modeled. Bernstein flags the financial equivalence: one HNA exposure = two Low-NA exposures, and ASML revenue per exposure is exactly 2x, so HNA push-out is dilutive to ASML near-term but financially neutral over the long arc. Market is treating this as a structural headwind; analysts argue that reading is overdone.

## 4. NAND / KV-Cache Structural Recovery (Not a Cyclical Bounce)

**Established:** 2026 Q2
**Sources:** GF -SNDK 3Q26 Preview.pdf, 2404 科技日报 英.PDF

NAND pricing and capacity discipline have re-rated on AI-inference KV-cache demand, which is now modeled as a structural (not cyclical) driver. SanDisk, SK Hynix, Samsung, and Kioxia are all expansion-disciplined; smartphone OEMs are paying $0.25/GB (up from $0.12/GB) and eSSD negotiations sit at $0.35–0.37/GB. GF Securities projects KV-cache adds ~100 EB of demand in 2027 (≈7% of total NAND) and potentially doubles in 2028 — making this the first time AI inference, not AI training, is set to move a commodity memory curve.

## 5. The CUDA Moat Is Narrowing at the Ecosystem and Library Layers

**Established:** 2026-04
**Sources:** 2404 科技日报 英.PDF (Fundstrat note)

Fundstrat's April 24 note — widely circulated in TMT Slack channels — argues CUDA's dominance is being re-priced at the margin across three layers. At the **ecosystem layer**, super-buyers (hyperscalers) are actively diversifying and a portion of developers are migrating, so de-concentration is already underway. At the **library layer**, Triton and TileLang enable cross-platform kernels, cuDNN / cuBLAS are no longer the sole path, and on select workloads AI-generated / optimized kernels are approaching or exceeding hand-written implementations. At the **compiler layer**, phase remains a critical checkpoint, but as per-token cost declines and generative methods advance, bypassing parts of the traditional compilation flow becomes plausible. This does not unwind NVIDIA's lead, but it re-opens a multi-year debate about the long-run CUDA premium.

## 6. The Physical-AI Capex Cycle Is Real but Stretched — Monetization Timelines Slipping

**Established:** 2026-04
**Sources:** 6.Tesla Inc North America Laying the Foundation.pdf (Morgan Stanley), 1.AMZN Everyone wants this to work.pdf (Bernstein), JPM_Intel_Better_Q1_Q2_o_2026-04-24_5276341.pdf

Multiple April prints signal the physical-AI buildout — robotaxi, humanoid robotics, on-prem foundries — is proceeding, but at slower cadence than consensus forecast. Tesla raised 2026 capex to >$25B but pushed robotaxi monetization back from "1H'26" to "preparations underway," delayed its AI5 chip, and slowed Optimus/Semi ramps. AWS is in a foundation-laying phase (power buildout: +1.2 GW in 2Q24, +3.9 GW in 2025, 2x capacity by 2027) with monetization capped near-term. The market is re-pricing payback periods on physical-AI infrastructure to longer-than-expected.
