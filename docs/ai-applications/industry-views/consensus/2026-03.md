# Industry Consensus · 2026-03

## 1. Agentic AI Is the Most Central Paradigm Shift Right Now

**Established:** 2026-03
**Sources:** Gartner forecast, NVIDIA GTC 2026, product directions across major vendors

The entire industry has converged on "Agentic AI" — AI capable of autonomously executing multi-step tasks — as the defining direction of this moment. Gartner predicts that by end of 2026, 40% of enterprise applications will embed task-oriented AI agents (vs. under 5% in 2025). The global Agentic AI market reached approximately $10.8 billion in March 2026 and is projected to hit $251 billion by 2034. The product strategies of OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and NVIDIA all revolve around agents and multi-agent collaboration.

## 2. AI Is a Five-Layer Cake: A Complete Infrastructure Stack from Energy to Applications

**Established:** 2026-01 (Davos), 2026-03 (confirmed at GTC and in blog posts)
**Sources:** Jensen Huang's fireside chat with BlackRock CEO Larry Fink (WEF Davos, 2026-01-21); NVIDIA blog post (2026-03-10); GTC 2026 keynote (2026-03)

Jensen Huang proposed that AI is a "Five-Layer Cake" infrastructure system — not a clever application or a single model. Every successful layer of application depends on every layer beneath it, all the way down to the power plants that keep it running.

**The five layers:**

1. **Energy**: The foundational layer. "The real-time generation of intelligence requires the real-time supply of electricity."
2. **Chips**: Processors that convert energy into large-scale computation, requiring high-bandwidth memory and high-speed interconnects.
3. **Infrastructure**: The physical system of land, power, cooling, networking, and orchestration — the "AI factory."
4. **Models**: AI systems that understand language, biology, chemistry, physics, robotics, and more.
5. **Applications**: The top layer, where economic value is ultimately realized. "This top layer is where the economic benefit actually happens."

Huang characterized this as "the largest infrastructure buildout in human history," adding: "We are just getting started — we've invested hundreds of billions so far, but there are trillions more of infrastructure to be built."

## 3. AI Compute Demand Has Triggered a Systemic Semiconductor Supply Chain Shortage — From Fab Capacity to Memory and PCB Substrates

**Established:** Second half of 2025; confirmed by multiple institutions in March 2026
**Sources:** SemiAnalysis (2026-03-14), Goldman Sachs GTC analysis (2026-03-16), UBS GTC preview (2026-03-09), Morgan Stanley (2026-03-17), Global Memory Tech (BofA, 2026-03-20), Taiwan CCL/PCB (Goldman Sachs, 2026-03-20), Micron 2QFY26 (Bernstein, 2026-03-19)

The critical bottleneck for AI expansion is no longer power — it is semiconductor fabrication capacity and the supply capacity of the entire supply chain. Shortages have propagated from upstream wafer manufacturing through memory, packaging, and PCB substrates, creating systemic pressure.

**Wafer fabrication:** AI workloads are expected to account for 86% of TSMC's N3 node capacity in 2027 (vs. ~60% in 2026). ASML produces roughly 70 EUV machines per year, rising to ~100 by end of decade — capacity expansion is measured in years.

**HBM and memory:** Described by SemiAnalysis as "a once-in-forty-years structural shortage." DRAM spot prices (16Gb DDR5) are up 600%+ year-over-year; NAND (1Tb wafer) up 400%+. Demand is growing exponentially (NVIDIA DGX B200 cluster HBM capacity: 1,440 GB; Google Ironwood TPU SuperPod: 1.77 PB), while supply is constrained to linear growth due to TSV stacking and CoWoS packaging bottlenecks. Multiple institutions estimate the shortage will persist through ~2030.

**PCB and substrates:** Lead times for high-end Taiwan CCL (copper-clad laminate) and ABF substrates have exceeded 20 weeks. Low-end CCL prices rose 30% month-over-month in April 2026, with another round of price increases expected in H2; substrate CCL gross margins of 40%+ far exceed the 22–30% typical of mainstream products.

**Key beneficiaries:** ASML, TSMC, SK Hynix, Micron, Samsung, and Taiwan's CCL/PCB supply chain.
