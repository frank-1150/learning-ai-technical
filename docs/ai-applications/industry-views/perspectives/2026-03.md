# Industry Perspectives · 2026-03

## Sam Altman · OpenAI CEO

**Sources:** CNBC (2026-03-03, 2026-03-12, 2026-03-22)

**On AI and the military:** After members of Congress (including Senator Mark Kelly) raised questions about OpenAI's Pentagon agreement, Altman replied on X: "Prohibitions on domestic mass surveillance and human responsibility for the use of force, including for autonomous weapon systems, are two of our most important safety principles, and the DoD has written these principles into the contract." At an internal all-hands, he stated that military "operational decisions" are "the government's responsibility."

> "Prohibitions on domestic mass surveillance and human responsibility for the use of force, including for autonomous weapon systems, are two of our most important safety principles."

**On AI infrastructure:** At the BlackRock US Infrastructure Summit, Altman acknowledged "all kinds of things go wrong at this scale," referencing a severe weather event at the Abilene, Texas data center campus.

**On talent expansion:** OpenAI plans to roughly double headcount from ~4,500 to ~8,000 employees in 2026 to cope with competitive pressure.

## Dario Amodei · Anthropic CEO (Dwarkesh Podcast long-form interview)

**Source:** Dwarkesh Podcast (2026-02-14)

This interview is the most technically detailed public discussion Amodei has given to date. The central thesis: **the exponential curve is approaching its endpoint, and the public has not yet realized this.**

**On the pace of technical progress:** Underlying progress is largely in line with expectations — moving from "smart high schooler" → "smart college student" → "PhD level," with coding ability now surpassing humans. The biggest surprise is not the progress itself, but how severely public perception lags behind reality.

**On the "Big Blob of Compute" hypothesis:** The framework Amodei proposed in 2017 still holds — compute volume, data volume, data quality, training duration, and the scalability of the objective function are the key variables. Both pre-training and reinforcement learning exhibit log-linear scaling.

**On Anthropic's revenue trajectory:**
- 2023: $0 → $100M
- 2024: $100M → $1B
- 2025: $1B → $9–10B
- January 2026: added several billion in a single month

**On industry compute scale projections:**
- 2026: ~10–15 GW
- 2027: ~30–40 GW
- 2028: ~100 GW
- 2029: ~300 GW

**On AI productivity gains:** Current AI delivers roughly 15–20% total factor productivity improvement (vs. ~5% six months ago), and still accelerating. Internally, some Anthropic engineers "write no code at all" — Claude does everything. Internally, 90% of code is now written by AI.

**On specific 1–3 year predictions:** AI can perform jobs requiring accumulated context knowledge (equivalent to a human employee who has been in the role 6 months), such as video editing; by end of 2026 or early 2027, AI systems will have Nobel laureate-level intellectual ability and be able to operate real-world interfaces; industry annual revenue will reach the trillion-dollar range by 2028–2030.

## Dario Amodei · Anthropic CEO (public talks roundup)

**Sources:** Morgan Stanley conference (2026-03), TechCrunch (2026-03-04, 2026-03-05), Futurism, Fortune (2026-01-21), WEF Davos 2026

**On Scaling Laws:** At the Morgan Stanley conference, Amodei said Scaling Laws "have not hit a wall" and that 2026 will see "radical acceleration." He noted that AI models are now making progress on unsolved mathematical problems, and that "some of the strongest engineers I've seen hand off virtually all programming work to AI."

**On AGI timelines:** At Davos 2026, Amodei described cognitive capabilities doubling every 4–12 months and stated that AI "could surpass humans in almost all respects within 1–2 years." He also warned that AI could threaten up to 50% of entry-level office jobs within 1–5 years — "but this information is not being communicated to the public."

**On OpenAI's military deals:** Amodei was reported to have called OpenAI's stance on military agreements "an outright lie," characterizing his own approach as "we actually care about preventing misuse" and criticizing OpenAI's position as "safety theater." Anthropic was reportedly placed on a US Department of Defense "supply chain risk" list for refusing to remove Claude's prohibitions on domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons use.

> "We don't know if the models are conscious... But we're open to the idea that it could be."

**On model consciousness:** Amodei publicly stated uncertainty about whether models are conscious. Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 system card includes a formal model welfare assessment for the first time.

## Jensen Huang · NVIDIA CEO

**Sources:** NVIDIA GTC 2026 (2026-03-17 to 2026-03-21), Fortune (2026-03-19), Bloomberg (2026-03-19), CNBC (2026-03-20)

**On Physical AI:** At GTC 2026, announced that "the ChatGPT moment for Physical AI has arrived," launching the Cosmos 3 world foundation model and GR00T N1.7 humanoid robot model, with GR00T N2 previewed. CES 2026 was widely described as the inflection point where "AI stepped off the screen and into the real world" — Audi, BMW, and other automakers are piloting humanoid robots in factories; NVIDIA has formed mass-production partnerships with Qualcomm, AMD, Intel, and robotics companies globally.

**On the strategic importance of Agentic AI:**

> "Every company in the world today needs to have an agentic system strategy. This is the new computer. This is as big of a deal as HTML, as big of a deal as Linux."

**On AI infrastructure investment:** Called this AI buildout "the largest infrastructure buildout in human history," projecting that orders for chips from Blackwell through Vera Rubin will reach $1 trillion before 2027.

**On AI and the workforce:** Predicts that in ten years NVIDIA will have ~75,000 human employees and ~7.5 million AI agents working in parallel (a 100:1 ratio), saying "AI won't replace workers — it will take over repetitive work."

**On AI panic:** Cautioned: "Warning is good; creating panic is not so good, because this technology is too important to us."

## Chamath Palihapitiya · Founder, Social Capital

**Source:** X (Twitter) posts, March 2026

**Core framework: Terminal Value Collapse — AI turns every moat into a temporary one**

Chamath proposes a capital markets re-pricing framework: AI dramatically lowers disruption costs and radically accelerates innovation speed, making it impossible for any company to reliably forecast free cash flow five years out — which breaks traditional DCF valuation models.

**Pricing framework (discounted by AI disruption probability):**
- 10% disruption probability/year (expected lifespan ~10 years) → ~6.5× FCF
- 20% disruption probability/year (expected lifespan ~5 years) → ~3.9× FCF
- 30% disruption probability/year (expected lifespan ~3.3 years) → ~2.8× FCF

(Traditional stable businesses without AI disruption risk typically trade at 10–12× FCF)

**Historical precedents:** The newspaper industry compressed from 15× EBITDA (2005) to 2–4× (2015); taxi medallion pricing blind spot before Uber's disruption in 2011.

**The core question:** Do you believe the most important companies in your portfolio have less than a 10% annual probability of being disrupted by AI?
