# Industry Perspectives · 2026-04

## Lip-Bu Tan · Intel CEO

**Sources:** INTEL_20260424_0406.pdf, 2404 科技日报 英.PDF (2026-04-23)

**On the AI stack and Intel's positioning:**

> "The next wave of AI will bring intelligence closer to the end user, moving from foundational models to inference to agentic. This shift is significantly increasing the need for Intel's CPUs and wafer and advanced packaging offerings."

Framing Q1'26 — where Intel delivered $13.58B revenue (+7.2% Y/Y), DC&AI grew 22% Y/Y, and gross margin surprised at 41.0% (vs Street 34.5%) — as validation of the thesis that as AI moves from centralized training to distributed inference and agentic workloads, CPU-side demand and packaging capacity become the binding constraints.

**Source:** JPM_Intel_Better_Q1_Q2_o_2026-04-24_5276341.pdf (2026-04-24)

## Fundstrat · NVIDIA / CUDA moat

**Source:** 2404 科技日报 英.PDF (2026-04-24)

**On CUDA re-pricing at the margin:**

> "Today, all three layers are being re-priced at the margin. At the ecosystem layer, super-buyers are actively diversifying, and a portion of developers are migrating — de-concentration is already underway. At the library layer, Triton, TileLang, and others enable developers to write kernels across platforms; cuDNN / cuBLAS are no longer the sole path, and on select workloads, AI-generated / optimized kernels are approaching or exceeding hand-written implementations. At the compiler layer, phase remains a critical checkpoint, but as per-token cost declines and generative methods advance, the possibility of bypassing parts of the traditional compilation flow is rising."

One of the most-circulated tactical notes of the month. Doesn't call a CUDA unwind, but argues the multi-year premium is back in play.
